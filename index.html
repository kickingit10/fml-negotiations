⚖ Fixed vs Variable: The Core Tradeoff

What does "fixed" vs "variable" mean? Fixed revenue (service charge, apparel, vehicles) is guaranteed every week regardless of how many packages you deliver. Variable revenue (per-stop, per-package, e-commerce, fuel, LP mix) goes up when volumes are strong but goes down if FedEx sends you fewer packages. The question is: do you want a bigger guaranteed check (MESO 1) or bet on your volumes staying strong (MESO 3)?

Should You Counter-Offer? Strategy

You don't have to accept a MESO as-is — here's what the data says about where to push back

Important context: FedEx's MESO process is designed so they profit regardless of which option you pick. Accepting a MESO is the starting point, not the final deal. If none of the MESOs meet your needs, you can decline all three and enter Traditional Negotiation — a round-by-round process where both sides make proposals. Industry consultants consistently advise that contractors who negotiate rather than simply accepting leave significantly more money on the table.

Key Negotiation Talking Points

1. E-Commerce Volume is Your Biggest Asset

Your e-commerce stops average 5,933/week — this is the majority of your workload and FedEx's fastest-growing segment. MESO 3's $1.96/stop e-com rate is the best offer, but push for $2.00+ to capture more of that value.

2. Fuel Surcharge Increase is Excessive

The fuel surcharge jumps from $0.20 to $0.28 per stop — a 40% increase across all 3 MESOs. Meanwhile, fuel prices have been relatively stable. Counter with $0.23 or request a fuel surcharge cap tied to actual DOE diesel index.

3. The "Liquidated Damage Rate" is New — Push Back

This penalty charge didn't exist in your current contract. It's pure downside risk with zero upside. Request it be reduced, capped annually, or removed entirely. At minimum, negotiate a grace period or threshold before it kicks in.

4. Service Charge Drop Needs Compensation

Your current service charge is $8,755/week. MESO 3 drops it to $6,015 — that's $2,740/week or ~$142,500/year less in guaranteed revenue. The higher variable rates compensate at current volumes, but if volumes dip, you need a safety net. Consider a volume guarantee clause.

5. LP Mix Charge is a Win in MESO 3

MESO 3 offers $1.50/LP pkg (up from current $1.00). With your LP mix at ~10%, this generates meaningful additional revenue. Lock this in and resist any reduction during counter-negotiation.

Bottom line strategy: Accept MESO 3 as your starting framework, but submit a counter-proposal targeting the fuel surcharge ($0.28 is aggressive), e-commerce stop rate (push above $2.00), and liquidated damage rate (new and unnecessary). Even small improvements on these three items could add $15,000-$40,000+ annually. If FedEx rejects the counter, MESO 3 is still the strongest option to accept before the deadline.
